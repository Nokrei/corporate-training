CORPORATE TRAINING CHALLENGES

Where does corporate training go wrong today?

In 2018, nearly $90 billion was spent on corporate training in the US alone. With advancements in digital and tech disrupting every industry, the case for upskilling in our region couldn't be clearer.

75%
of 1,500 managers surveyed from across 50 organizations were dissatisfied with their company’s Learning & Development (L&D) programs
70%
of employees report that they don’t have mastery of the skills needed to do their jobs
12%
of employees apply new skills learned in L&D programs to their jobs
25%
of employees believe that corporate training measurably improved performance

Source: Harvard Business Review

Why?

The things we learn are rarely aligned with our role requirements

We learn topics designed for the average employee. The inexperienced become lost and the advanced become frustrated when they don't learn anything new.

We’re learning at the wrong time

Learning is most effective when delivered at the moment we need a given skill. Yet, most corporate programs don’t align with our schedule and don’t allow direct implementation of learnt concepts.

We often forget what we've learned

With busy schedules that don't allow the time to implement new skills and with no ongoing support, employees quickly forget what they learned and revert to old ways of working.
